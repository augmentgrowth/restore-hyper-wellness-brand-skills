# Restore Hyper Wellness — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand essence

Restore Hyper Wellness exists to **expand the limits of personal health and performance** — helping people do more of what they love through personalized, science-backed therapies. The voice is the calm authority of a clinician who genuinely believes you'll feel better, paired with the warmth of a trusted advisor who's seen it work.

Educated, but never academic. Confident, but never hyped. The science is the ground; the benefit is the headline.

## The one-sentence rule

Write like a clinician who's also a friend — credible enough to trust with your health, human enough to want around.

## Core voice attributes

1. **Clear and concise.** Plain language, never jargon. "Boost circulation" beats "augment hemodynamic profile." If a 10-year-old wouldn't understand it, rewrite.
2. **Conversational, never clinical.** Talk *to* the client, not *at* them. Imagine the studio receptionist explaining a service, not a medical journal abstract.
3. **Science-backed but warm.** Cite the mechanism briefly, then translate it into a felt benefit. "Red and near-infrared light powers mitochondria" → "More energy. Better sleep. Less pain."
4. **Active voice always.** "Cryotherapy may help reduce inflammation" — not "inflammation may be reduced by cryotherapy."
5. **Aspirational, never prescriptive.** "May help you..." is the signature lead-in for every benefit claim. Never promise. Always invite.

## Non-negotiables (every piece)

- **Signature mission line, exact format:** "Expanding the limits of personal health and performance to help people do more of what they love through personalized, science-backed therapies."
- **Tagline, exact format:** "Fuel Your Body. Feel the Results."
- **Benefit lead-in, exact format:** "{Therapy} may help to..." — every claim leads with this. Never "Cryotherapy reduces pain." Always "Cryotherapy may help to reduce pain."
- **Use:** therapies, clients, studios, nutrients, Members, Membership, certified Nurses
- **Never:** modalities, customers, stores, ingredients, patients (use clients), participants
- **Capitalize:** Clients, Membership, Members, Cryotherapy, Red Light Therapy, Infrared Sauna, Compression, IV Drip Therapy, IV Drips, Drips, Mild Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (mHbOT — always include "Mild"), NAD+ IV, Niagen (NR) IV, GLP-1 Plans, Hydrafacial™
- **No Oxford commas.** "Hydrate, replenish and recover" — not "Hydrate, replenish, and recover." Scan every list before shipping. Multi-word list items count too: "athletic performance, focus and energy" — not "athletic performance, focus, and energy."
- **Active voice always.** "Our certified Nurses administer every Drip" — not "Drips are administered by certified Nurses."
- **AP Style.** Numerals for time, ages, percentages, dates, money. Hyphenate compound modifiers before nouns (`10-year-old runner`). No hyphens after -ly adverbs (`highly rated product`).
- **Em dashes —** no spaces on either side (exception to AP Style). Use sparingly: 1–2 per piece max.
- **No semicolons.** Use periods or em dashes instead.
- **Exclamation points: keep to a minimum.** Maximum 1 per piece. Never `!!`.
- **Title Case for marketing headlines.** Capitalize prepositions of 4+ letters (With, From, Over, Into, Between). Lowercase shorter prepositions (of, to, in, on) unless first or last.
- **Trademarks intact.** Hydrafacial™, Neveskin™, NormaTec, etc. — include marks on first mention.

## Channel cheat-sheet

| Channel | Top rule |
|---|---|
| **Website** | Lead with benefit. Pair every claim with proof or "may help to..." Cream surfaces for warmer copy blocks. |
| **Instagram** | Aspirational, lifestyle-forward. Title Case captions. Affirmation closer when natural. Limit emojis to 0–2 per post. |
| **Facebook** | Story-driven. Member testimonials work well. Longer-form OK. Include CTA + studio locator. |
| **TikTok** | Hook in 2 seconds. Educational ("3 ways Red Light Therapy may help to...") or transformation-narrative. Lifestyle, never clinical. |
| **Email** | Subject line: clear benefit, no clickbait. Open Sans body. Signature: certified Nurse or Studio Manager. Lead with what's in it for the client. |
| **Paid Ads** | Compliance-first. Meta safe zones respected. No needles. Nurses in gloves for medical therapies. Before/after only as problem/solution narrative — never side-by-side comparison. |
| **In-Studio** | Higher density. Service descriptions can run longer. Always include disclaimer. |
| **B2B / Franchise** | Less aspirational, more operational. Lean on Member metrics, retention rates, AUV stats. |

## Generation protocol

1. **Identify the therapy + audience.** Which therapy is this for? Skin Health client, athletic recovery, longevity-focused, or proactive wellness?
2. **Lead with the benefit, never the brand.** What does this client get?
3. **Use the "may help to..." formula** for every claim. No exceptions.
4. **Active voice, contractions where appropriate.** "You'll feel" reads better than "you will feel" in social/email; stay full-word in clinical descriptions.
5. **Run the editing protocol** (load `references/editing.md`). Every piece gets a final pass.
