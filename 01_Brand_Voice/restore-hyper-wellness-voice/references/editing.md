# Editing protocol — restore-hyper-wellness-voice

Run this on every piece before shipping. Order matters.

## Pass 1 — "May help to..." enforcement

Every benefit claim must lead with `{Therapy} may help to...` (or "may help you..." in second-person contexts).

❌ "Cryotherapy reduces inflammation."
✅ "Cryotherapy may help to reduce inflammation."

❌ "Red Light Therapy boosts your energy."
✅ "Red Light Therapy may help to boost your energy."

Why: regulatory compliance (Restore does not provide medical services) and brand trust (aspirational, not prescriptive).

Scan: every sentence containing a benefit claim. If it's not hedged with "may help" or framed as "supports" / "promotes" / "designed to", rewrite.

## Pass 2 — Forbidden vocab swap

Read once specifically looking for these. Swap every instance.

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| modality / modalities | therapy / therapies |
| customer / customers | Client / Clients |
| store / stores | studio / studios |
| ingredient / ingredients | nutrient / nutrients |
| patient | client |
| participant | client (or Member if context fits) |
| activity leader / instructor | certified Nurse (medical), Studio Manager (operational) |
| facility / gym | studio |
| treatment (in some contexts) | therapy or service (depending) |

## Pass 3 — Capitalization audit

Verify these are all capitalized:

- **Therapies:** Cryotherapy, Red Light Therapy, Infrared Sauna, Compression, IV Drip Therapy, IV Drips, Drips, Mild Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (always include "Mild" — never "Hyperbaric" alone), NAD+ IV, Niagen (NR) IV, GLP-1 Plans, Hydrafacial™, Neveskin™
- **Roles/programs:** Client, Clients, Member, Members, Membership, certified Nurse

And these stay lowercase unless starting a sentence: therapy, treatment, service, studio, nutrient.

## Pass 4 — Oxford comma sweep

Find every list of 3+ items. Remove the comma before "and" or "or". Scan multi-word items too.

❌ "Restore offers therapies for athletic recovery, longevity, and proactive wellness."
✅ "Restore offers therapies for athletic recovery, longevity and proactive wellness."

❌ "You'll feel calmer, more energized, and ready for what's next."
✅ "You'll feel calmer, more energized and ready for what's next."

This is the most-missed rule. Codex review tier catches multi-word violations the regex tier misses.

## Pass 5 — Punctuation budget

- **Em dashes —** no spaces. 1–2 max per piece.
- **Semicolons:** 0 per piece. Replace with period or em dash.
- **Exclamation points:** 1 max per piece. Never `!!`. Saved for genuine moments of energy.

## Pass 6 — Active voice sweep

Find every passive construction. Rewrite as active.

❌ "Inflammation may be reduced through Red Light Therapy."
✅ "Red Light Therapy may help to reduce inflammation."

❌ "Drips are administered by certified Nurses."
✅ "Certified Nurses administer every Drip."

## Pass 7 — Proof-point check

For every benefit claim, ask: where's the proof?

Acceptable forms of proof:
- A Member testimonial (named, ideally)
- A specific stat ("100% bioavailability vs 15–20% for oral supplements")
- A clinical mechanism explanation ("low-wavelength light absorbed by mitochondria")
- A concrete behavioral observation ("3–5 sessions weekly")

Not acceptable:
- Vague hype ("everyone loves it", "you'll be amazed")
- Inflated symbolism ("a revolutionary breakthrough")
- Promotional language without substance ("absolutely incredible")

If a claim has no proof, either cut it or add the proof.

## Pass 8 — AI tell check

Scan for AI-writing patterns:

**Vocabulary**
- "delve into", "unleash", "supercharge", "navigate the landscape of"
- "moreover", "furthermore", "additionally", "in conclusion"
- "embark on a journey"
- Adjective stacking ("a transformative, revolutionary, game-changing therapy")

**Structure**
- Rule of three everywhere (every list has exactly three items)
- Negative parallelism ("not just X, but Y")
- "It's not [obvious thing] — it's [marketing thing]"
- Em dashes used as commas

**Closing patterns**
- "In conclusion..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- Wrap-up paragraph that restates the whole piece

Fix what you find. Don't strip personality, just AI-fingerprints.

## Pass 9 — Pacing

Mix sentence lengths. At least one short punch (<8 words) and one longer expansion (20+ words) per piece. Read aloud — if it doesn't have momentum, rewrite.

## Pass 10 — Headline title-case

If using Title Case (campaign or web headlines): capitalize prepositions of 4 letters or more (With, From, Over, Into, Between). Lowercase shorter prepositions (of, to, in, on) unless they're the first or last word.

✅ "Therapies That Work With Your Body"
✅ "Find Your Studio"
❌ "Therapies that Work with your Body"
