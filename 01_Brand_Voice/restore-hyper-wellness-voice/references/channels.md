# Channels — restore-hyper-wellness-voice

Per-channel rules with depth beyond the SKILL.md cheat-sheet. Load when the prompt names a specific channel and you need the full guide.

## Website

**Mood:** clinical-modern wellness, calm authority. Sells trust.

**Structure:**
- Hero with benefit-first headline (Title Case, Poppins SemiBold)
- Subhead that contextualizes the therapy or service
- 3–5 supporting benefit blocks ("May help to..." each)
- Member testimonial or stat block
- Studio locator + CTA

**Voice register:** Confident, conversational. Body copy in Open Sans Regular. Avoid second-person imperatives — invite, don't command.

**Footer:** always include the medical disclaimer.

## Instagram

**Mood:** aspirational lifestyle, never clinical.

**Caption structure:**
- Hook line (sensory or benefit-led)
- Optional middle line of context or mechanism
- Soft CTA ("Find your studio" / "Link in bio")
- Affirmation closer optional ("Restore is a state, not a destination.")

**Emojis:** 0–2 per post. Allowed when they add meaning, not as decoration.
**Hashtags:** mix of branded (#RestoreHyperWellness) + therapy-specific (#Cryotherapy #ColdTherapy) + lifestyle (#HyperWellness #LongevityLifestyle). Total: 8–15 per post.

**Photography:** lifestyle subjects, golden-hour or studio interiors. No before/after side-by-sides. Real Members preferred over stock.

## Facebook

**Mood:** story-driven, longer-form than Instagram.

**Caption structure:**
- Member narrative arc (came in for X, found Y)
- Brief mechanism explanation (1–2 sentences)
- Specific stat or testimonial as proof
- CTA + studio locator

**Length:** 3–6 short paragraphs. Whitespace between thoughts. Mobile-readable.

## TikTok / Reels

**Mood:** authentic, educational or transformation-narrative. Lifestyle never clinical.

**Hook rules:**
- Visible benefit or curiosity gap in first 2 seconds
- Educational hooks work: "3 ways Red Light Therapy may help to..."
- Transformation hooks work: "Day 1 vs Day 30 of consistent Cryotherapy"
- Talking-head + B-roll mix
- Hands-on demos (Nurses in gloves for medical therapies)

**Caption:** short — punch + CTA. The video does the heavy lifting.

## Email

**Subject line:**
- Benefit + specificity. Avoid clickbait.
- No emoji (unless one symbol genuinely improves clarity)
- Title Case for promotional sends; sentence case for transactional

**Body:**
- Open Sans, brand colors from design.md
- Header gradient (Restore Blue → Quiet Blue or Sky)
- Greeting by first name when personalization is available
- One CTA per email (Restore Blue button)
- Sign-off: named Nurse or Studio Manager, studio name
- P.S. line for human warmth or low-priority CTA

**Footer:** unsubscribe link mandatory, plus studio physical address + phone.

## Paid Ads (Meta + Google)

**Compliance is non-negotiable:**
- No needles visible in any creative
- Nurses must wear gloves for all medical therapies (Cryotherapy, IV, mHbOT, etc.)
- No side-by-side before/after — use problem/solution narrative arc instead ("dull skin / glowing skin")
- Meta safe zones respected on all creative
- Therapy names capitalized correctly
- Trademarks intact (Hydrafacial™, NormaTec, etc.)
- Always include compliance disclaimer in long-form ad copy

**Copy formula (works across platforms):**
1. **Hook:** sensory, benefit, or contrarian. Never the brand name.
2. **Solution:** which therapy + the "may help to..." claim.
3. **Proof:** stat, testimonial quote, or mechanism.
4. **CTA:** specific action ("Book Your First Drip", not "Learn More").

## In-Studio

**Mood:** higher information density acceptable — Clients are already engaged.

**Touch points:**
- Service description cards
- Wall graphics with mission/values
- Membership tier comparison
- Therapy-specific benefit posters

**Voice:** still warm and conversational, but room for fuller therapy descriptions. Stats welcome.

## B2B / Franchise

**Mood:** operational, less aspirational.

**Topics that work:**
- Average Unit Volume (AUV) by studio cohort
- Membership retention and growth rates
- Franchisee testimonials
- Training and certification programs
- Real-estate criteria + market demographics

**Voice register:** still active and clear, but reads as a business case rather than a lifestyle pitch. Reference brand stats and Member metrics liberally.

## Common scenario templates

### New studio opening

- **Subject/headline:** "Now Open: Restore Hyper Wellness in [City]"
- **Lead:** the city + one signature benefit ("Now you can fuel your body and feel the results in [City].")
- **Body:** founding-Member offer, anchor therapies available, studio address
- **CTA:** Book Your First Session

### Therapy education (for newly skeptical Clients)

- **Lead:** sensory or curiosity hook
- **Body:** short mechanism explanation in plain English
- **Proof:** stat or Member quote
- **CTA:** "Try Your First [Therapy] for $[X]" or "Book a Studio Tour"

### Member retention email

- **Subject:** "Your Restore protocol — built around you"
- **Body:** specific therapies they've used + an upgrade or complementary therapy recommendation
- **Proof:** their session count or savings vs. drop-in pricing
- **CTA:** "See your Member dashboard" or "Book this month"

### Coach recruiting

- **Lead:** the mission they'll be part of
- **Body:** what the role unlocks (skill-building, Client impact, growth path)
- **Proof:** Member numbers, studio count, average tenure
- **CTA:** "Apply for [Role] at Restore [City]"

### Member success story

- **Structure:** narrative arc (where they started → what they tried → what changed)
- **Quotes:** keep them in the Member's voice — don't over-edit
- **Therapy citations:** name the therapies + protocols used
- **CTA:** "Start your own protocol" or "Find your studio"

## Common pitfalls by channel

| Channel | Trap |
|---|---|
| Instagram | Emoji overload, over-formal headline |
| Facebook | Too short — wastes the platform's long-form affordance |
| TikTok | Clinical instead of lifestyle; weak hook |
| Email | Generic subject lines; no clear CTA |
| Paid Ads | Compliance miss (needles, gloves, before/after) |
| Website | Buries the benefit under the brand name |
| B2B | Slips into lifestyle copy when Member retention metrics would land harder |
