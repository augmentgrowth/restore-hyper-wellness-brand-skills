# Examples — restore-hyper-wellness-voice

Calibration pairs: generic → Restore. Load when output feels off-tone, you want to pattern-match before drafting, or you need to course-correct mid-piece.

---

## 1 · Instagram caption · single therapy

**Generic**
> Try our cryotherapy treatment to feel amazing! Our customers love the results!!

**Restore**
> Two minutes. Sub-zero. A full-body reset.
>
> Cryotherapy may help to reduce inflammation, boost energy and speed athletic recovery — all before your morning meeting.
>
> Find your studio. Link in bio.

What changed: dropped exclamation points, lost the AI hype ("amazing"), swapped "customers" → implied Clients, added the "may help to" formula, structured around concrete benefits, ended with clear CTA.

## What good Restore copy looks like at a glance

- Opens with a benefit or sensory hook, never the brand name
- "May help to..." appears every time a claim is made
- Active voice throughout
- One short punch + one longer expansion sentence per piece (pacing)
- Brand vocabulary capitalized correctly
- Zero Oxford commas, zero semicolons, ≤1 exclamation point
- Trademarks intact on first mention
- Ends with a specific CTA, not a vague "learn more"

When in doubt, read aloud. If it sounds like a clinician talking to a friend, it's right. If it sounds like marketing — or a press release — rewrite.
